Monalisa Verma: 20122350

Phishing, a type of identity theft, is one of the most common forms of online crime. Phishing/Hacking is an unlawful access into a computer or an online system. As technology is the future and will continue to evolve, the risks of losing personal information will be far higher as well. Fake attachments and illegal/invalid domains pose a great risk as users increase online usage. The global pandemic of COVID-19 is a key factor in the increase of online shopping, usage of online platforms to communicate or educate online. This has led users to enter their personal details on several websites and platforms online increasing their risk of phishing. There are many ways or right actions that can be taken in order to prevent phishing and other forms of online identity theft. There should be an equal opportunity for the whole online user society to get educated about the effects of phishing and ways to keep them safe online. If online users are aware of ways their data and personal information can be stolen, they will be more cautious and therefore, will avoid the risks of data theft while using technology online. I would strongly recommend to organise frequent events, to teach people about risk factors. Ethical issues play an important role while governing the online society and there are a few ethical issues regarding identity theft. Ethical principles are the standards used to govern the effective and safe usage of online information. These ethical issues should also be addressed in the events while educating society. Phishing is a crime, let's come forward and protect our privacy/p>

So if people begin and or continue to be informed about the risks that we face when dealing with phishing and identity theft and even technology as a whole then they will become more aware which will reduce the amount of successful phishing attacks and also it will help people to cope with other types of cyber attacks. Overall if people become more aware about phishing then risks of phishing will decrease and the opportunities and choices will increase and will allow them to teach themselves and others as well as the online environment will become a little bit more safer for everyone.

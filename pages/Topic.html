Identity theft is made possible by modern technological systems and continuous advancement as customers are purchasing goods from modern payment systems, risking their PII.

It is when a person obtains data or documents belonging to the victim and misuses the data obtained. Identity theft also may mean the person (criminal) obtaining the victims personal information and misusing it

According to a report published recently from 2013 to 2016, phishing is a scam that caused up to $3.2 billion in damages. It affected more than 79 countries. Almost 17,000 businesses were affected through phishing attacks.

A common form of identity theft is phishing. Phishing is a type of social engineering attack which is often used to steal user data. For example, user data, credit card numbers and emails. These attacks can have long-term, devastating impacts for individuals. There are forms within phishing as well. A form of this is spear phishing. Imperva (web-site) targets a specific person or enterprise. It is a more in-depth version of phishing that normally requires special information of a business/organisation.
